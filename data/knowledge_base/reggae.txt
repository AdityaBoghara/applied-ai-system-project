Reggae originated in Jamaica and is defined by its offbeat guitar "skank," deep bass lines, and relaxed, syncopated rhythms that create a distinctive rolling groove. The energy is low to medium — unhurried and grounded, inviting movement that is sway-based rather than high-intensity. The mood is uplifting, spiritual, and socially conscious, often addressing themes of peace, unity, Rastafarian faith, and resistance to oppression. Vocals are warm and melodic, frequently accompanied by dub effects and layered harmonies. Typical listeners are laid-back, socially aware individuals who appreciate music that combines positive vibes with meaningful messages.
